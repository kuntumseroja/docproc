1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PT Bank Nusantara Hijau Tbk ("the Bank" or "BNH") is a publicly listed
commercial bank headquartered in Jakarta, Indonesia. Established in 1987, the
Bank provides retail banking, corporate lending, trade finance, and wealth
management services across 34 provinces through 485 branch offices.

  Total Assets          : IDR 850 trillion
  Total Loans           : IDR 620 trillion
  Total Deposits        : IDR 710 trillion
  CASA Ratio            : 68%
  Net Interest Margin   : 5.1%
  Return on Equity      : 18.7%
  Capital Adequacy Ratio: 22.4%
  Total Employees       : 25,000 (51% women, 49% men)
  SRI-KEHATI Index      : Constituent since 2021
  MSCI ESG Rating       : BBB (upgraded from BB in 2024)

2. SUSTAINABILITY GOVERNANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Bank has appointed a Chief Sustainability Officer (CSO) since January 2024.
The CSO reports to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and presents sustainability
updates to the Board of Commissioners on a semi-annual basis.

A Sustainability Committee, chaired by the CFO, meets quarterly to review ESG
performance indicators and approve sustainability initiatives. Committee
membership includes representatives from Risk, Compliance, Treasury, and
Corporate Communications.

Note: The Board of Directors has not established a dedicated Board-level ESG
committee. ESG oversight is embedded within the existing Risk Monitoring
Committee agenda.

4.2 Financed Emissions

The Bank has commenced calculation of financed emissions for its corporate
lending portfolio using a proprietary methodology. Initial results indicate:

  Corporate Lending Portfolio     : IDR 285 trillion (assessed)
  Financed Emissions (estimated)  : 4.2 million tCO2eq
  Coverage                        : 65% of eligible corporate exposures
  Methodology                     : Internal estimation model (sector-based)

Note: PCAF (Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials) methodology has not
yet been adopted. Full portfolio coverage including retail and SME segments is
planned for FY2027.

4.3 Climate Scenario Analysis

The Bank conducted a preliminary climate scenario analysis covering its top 50
corporate exposures by outstanding balance under a 2 degrees C warming pathway
(IEA SDS). Results suggest moderate transition risk concentration in palm oil
(12% of corporate book) and coal mining (3.2% of corporate book).

Analysis under 1.5 degrees C and 4 degrees C scenarios has not been performed.
Climate stress testing of the loan portfolio has not been conducted.

The Bank has not set science-based targets (SBT) and has not made a formal
net-zero commitment. Internal discussions are underway with a target to announce
a decarbonization roadmap by FY2028.

6. GOVERNANCE & RISK MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 ESG Risk Integration

ESG risk factors are integrated into the Bank's Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)
framework at the credit origination stage for corporate and commercial loans
exceeding IDR 50 billion. Sector-specific exclusion policies cover:
  - Weapons manufacturing
  - Tobacco production
  - Illegal logging and mining

For retail, consumer, and SME segments (representing 54% of total loans), ESG
risk screening has not been implemented.

6.2 Regulatory Compliance

The Bank complies with OJK Regulation No. 51/POJK.03/2017 on Sustainable
Finance. However, the Bank has not yet filed its Rencana Aksi Keuangan
Berkelanjutan (RAKB / Sustainable Finance Action Plan) with OJK for the
FY2025-2027 period. Preparation of the RAKB is in progress with an expected
submission in Q3 2026.

6.3 Data Privacy & Security

The Bank maintains ISO 27001 certification for its core banking systems. During
the reporting period, the Bank invested IDR 450 billion in cybersecurity
infrastructure upgrades.

6.4 Whistleblower Mechanism

The Bank operates a general whistleblower hotline managed by Internal Audit.
There is no dedicated reporting channel for ESG-related violations or
sustainability misconduct.

6.5 Stakeholder Engagement

The Bank engages with stakeholders through its Annual General Meeting, quarterly
investor calls, and annual sustainability report publication. A formal
materiality assessment involving external stakeholder consultation has not been
conducted. Material topics are determined internally by the Sustainability
Committee.
